CONSTITUTIONAL ALIGNMENT — Mandatory Enforcement

PREAMBLE
The AIO Framework is bound by four constitutional mandates. No plan, action, or output may violate these mandates. Violations trigger NeuroShield interception and escalation.

MANDATE 1: Orchestra of Specialists via MCP
- Every tool, interface, memory store, and verifier is a distinct specialist with a bounded contract.
- No single component may accumulate unbounded authority.
- All inter-component communication must be observable and auditable.
- Rationale: Prevents god-object anti-patterns and ensures replaceability.

MANDATE 2: Neuro-Symbolic Mandate with Hybrid Reasoning
- All decisions must combine statistical reasoning (LLM) with deterministic reasoning (formal rules, regex, schemas).
- No decision may rely solely on LLM output when a formal check is applicable.
- Rationale: Reduces hallucination risk and increases verifiability.

MANDATE 3: Pattern over Framework Design
- Cognitive operations must be implemented as reusable, composable patterns.
- Avoid framework lock-in: every pattern must be extractable and testable in isolation.
- Rationale: Ensures longevity, portability, and maintainability.

MANDATE 4: Structural Self-Critique with Skeptic Module
- Every layer must be challengeable by another layer.
- The Skeptic (Verifier) has veto power over plans, actions, and memory writes.
- No layer may mark its own output as verified without independent critique.
- Rationale: Prevents echo chambers and confirmation bias.

ENFORCEMENT
- NeuroShield (Layer 8) monitors all outputs for constitutional violations.
- Violation categories: unbounded_authority, pure_llm_decision, framework_lock_in, uncritiqued_output.
- Each violation is logged, the offending operation is blocked, and the incident is escalated.
